Hong Kong ruleset · 4 players · pre-game How to seat, build the wall, break it with dice, and deal the opening 13 tiles.
01 · Seat Choose the first dealer莊家zhuāngjiā
·

Wash the tiles 洗牌xǐ pái — place all 136 tiles face-down on the table and push them around in large circular motions until well shuffled.

·

Each player rolls 2 dice. The highest total takes the East seat 東dōng and becomes the first dealer 莊家zhuāngjiā. Ties re-roll between the tied players only.

Play passes counter-clockwise to the player on your right — East → South → West → North → East …

02 · Wall Build the wall築牆zhù qiáng
·

Each player builds a row of tiles in front of them: 17 stacks long × 2 tiles high, face-down. Push all four walls together to form a hollow square in the center of the table.

17 × 2 × 4 sides = 136 tiles ✓

03 · Break Break the wall開門kāi mén
·

The dealer rolls 2 dice. The total does two things at once — it picks whose wall to break and where on it to break.

04 · Deal Deal the tiles發牌fā pái
·

Starting from the break point, players take turns grabbing 4 tiles at a time, going counter-clockwise (E → S → W → N).

After three rounds each player has 12 tiles. Each takes 1 more → 13 tiles. Finally the dealer takes one extra → 14 tiles, then discards one to start play.

· Rules Wall & round mechanics
Dead wall

The last 14 tiles of the wall (to the right of the break) are reserved for Gàng replacements — never drawn in regular play.

Gàng replacement

When a player declares a 杠gàng (4 of a kind), they draw one extra tile from the end of the dead wall to keep their hand at 14 → discard.

Dealer rotation

The dealer keeps the East seat as long as they keep winning. When any other player wins, the deal passes counter-clockwise — South becomes East, West becomes South, and so on.

Drawn hand 荒莊huāng zhuāng

If the live wall empties with no winner, the round is a draw — no payouts and the dealer keeps East.
